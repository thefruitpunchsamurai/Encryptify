Symmetric key encryption is a method of encryption that uses a single key to both encrypt and decrypt the data. This type of encryption is commonly used in situations where both the sender and receiver of the data are trusted parties who share the same key. Public-key encryption, on the other hand, uses two different keys - a public key and a private key - to encrypt and decrypt data. This type of encryption is often used in situations where the sender and receiver of the data are not necessarily trusted parties.

One-time pad encryption is an unbreakable encryption technique that uses a randomly generated key that is only used once. This ensures that even if someone were to intercept the encrypted data, they would not be able to decrypt it without access to the original key.
